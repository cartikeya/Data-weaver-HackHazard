Deep work is the ability to focus without distraction on a cognitively demanding task, and it's becoming increasingly valuable in a world filled with distractions, where people are constantly connected to their devices and social media, and where the ability to multitask is often mistakenly seen as a virtue. The concept of deep work was first introduced by Cal Newport, a professor of computer science at Georgetown University, who argues that the ability to perform deep work is becoming increasingly rare, and that this is having a negative impact on our productivity, our creativity, and our overall well-being. Newport argues that deep work is essential for mastering complex skills, and for making significant contributions to one's field, and that it's necessary to create a schedule that allows for large blocks of uninterrupted time, in order to engage in deep work.

The concept of deep work is closely related to the idea of deliberate practice, which was first introduced by K. Anders Ericsson, a professor at Florida State University. Deliberate practice refers to the process of focusing on a specific task or skill, and working to improve it through dedicated and structured practice. Ericsson's research has shown that deliberate practice is essential for becoming an expert in any field, and that it's the key to achieving mastery. The idea of deliberate practice is closely related to the idea of deep work, because it requires the ability to focus without distraction on a specific task or skill, and to work on it for extended periods of time.
